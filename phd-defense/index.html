Research Overview

The Work

Dams & Reservoirs
Satellites
AI & Machine Learning
Global Scale

Every major dam in the world is slowly filling up with mud — and almost nobody is tracking it.

Rivers carry sediment downstream and deposit it in reservoirs over time, quietly eroding the water storage we rely on for drinking, farming, and electricity. This research uses satellite imagery and machine learning to monitor any reservoir across the globe — detecting this invisible loss without expensive on-the-ground surveys, and giving water managers the tools to plan before it's too late.

Reservoirs formed by dams underpin global water, food, and energy security, yet their long-term viability is increasingly threatened by sedimentation — the gradual infilling of storage capacity by deposited sediment.

This dissertation develops an integrated framework combining satellite remote sensing, hydrological modeling, machine learning, and curated field observations:

  • RAT 3.0: Scalable open-source system estimating reservoir dynamics from satellite observations
  • GRILSS: Global dataset of sedimentation rates for 1,000+ reservoirs
  • RECLAIM: Machine learning model with R²=0.86 estimating sedimentation rates
  • GLoRS: First global assessment of reservoir sedimentation (1984–2023)

Major reservoirs are losing storage at ~0.42% per year. These tools enable global-scale estimation without costly surveys, supporting resilient water infrastructure planning.
